Although I already learned how to program using basic data structures and algorithms in previous CS courses, it wasn't until the 2020-2021 academic year that I truly started to get a sense of all the joys and complexities of software engineering. My education that year really could be described as "learning by building things". This was the idea that the best way to understand a new concept was to apply it to a project. In doing so, having to think about things like edge cases, error handling and performance optimizations naturally led to a deeper understanding that I would never have been able to achieve just by reading notes. Over the course of that year, I transitioned from being knowing little of web development to being able to appreciate the details of things like asynchronous Javascript, SQL query optimization and the TCP protocol - simply because understanding these details were crucial for my projects to work. This active style of learning appealed to me more than anything I had ever done before, and quickly became one of my favourite aspects of programming.
